Welcome to TowerEater. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data is collected, how it is used, and your rights regarding that data.

The monster does not remember its past lives. Neither do we store yours.

Information We Collect

TowerEater stores your game progress and preferences locally on your device. We do not collect personally identifiable information directly. The following data is stored locally on your device:

  • Your current level and highest level reached
  • Sound and music preferences
  • Session data
  • No account information or personal data

Advertising

This app displays advertisements provided by Google AdMob, a third-party advertising service. AdMob may automatically collect limited technical information, including:

  • Device type and operating system
  • Advertising identifiers (e.g., Google Advertising ID)
  • Crash and performance data
  • General usage and interaction information

This information is collected and processed by Google in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy. You may opt out of personalized advertising through your device settings.

Data Storage

All game progress is stored locally on your device only. We do not:

  • Transmit your personal data to our servers
  • Sell or share your personal information with third parties
  • Require you to create an account or register
  • Collect any sensitive personal information

You can delete all locally stored data at any time through your device or browser settings. Your progress is yours alone.

Third-Party Services

The application may integrate with the following third-party services, each governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Google Play Services
  • Google AdMob

Data Safety

💾 Game data stored locally on your device only
🔒 No sensitive personal information collected by us
🚫 No data sold or shared with third parties
🗑️ Clear all local data anytime via device settings

Children's Privacy

TowerEater does not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.
